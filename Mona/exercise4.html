French theatrical release poster French Léon Directed by Luc Besson Written by Luc Besson Produced by Patrice Ledoux Starring Jean Reno Gary Oldman Natalie Portman Danny Aiello Cinematography Thierry Arbogast Edited by Sylvie Landra Music by Éric Serra Production company Les Films du Dauphin[1] Distributed by Gaumont Buena Vista International[1] Release date 14 September 1994 (France)[2] Running time 110 minutes Country France Language English Budget $16 million[3] Box office $46.1 million[4] Léon: The Professional (French: Léon), titled Leon in the UK and Australia (and originally titled The Professional in the US), is a 1994 English-language French action-thriller film[5][6][7][8] written and directed by Luc Besson. It stars Jean Reno and Gary Oldman, and features the film debut of Natalie Portman. The plot follows Léon (Reno), a professional hitman, who reluctantly takes in twelve-year-old Mathilda (Portman) after her family is murdered by corrupt Drug Enforcement Administration agent Norman Stansfield (Oldman). Léon and Mathilda form an unusual relationship, as she becomes his protégée and learns the hitman's trade.
